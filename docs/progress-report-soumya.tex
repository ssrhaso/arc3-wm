\section{Motivation and gap}
ARC-AGI-3 is a benchmark of 25 public interactive abstract-reasoning games in
which the rules and goals are \emph{not} given and must be inferred through
active play. It is scored by RHAE (Relative Human Action Efficiency): per level,
$s_i=\min\big((\text{human\_actions}/\text{ai\_actions})^2,\,1.15\big)$; a game
score is the level-index-weighted mean $\sum_i s_i\, i / \sum_i i$ over all
levels (including uncompleted ones), and the total score averages games equally.
The ARC-AGI living survey~\cite{survey2026} reviews $\sim$80 papers across
ARC-AGI-1/2/3, documents a consistent $\sim$3$\times$ performance cliff from
ARC-AGI-1 to ARC-AGI-3, finds only 3 of 80 papers report any ARC-AGI-3 result,
\emph{zero} of them world-model based, and explicitly names world-model
induction as the required next step. Ying et al.~\cite{ying2025} argue the same
from first principles: today's evaluations test static pre-trained
representations, whereas the human capability of interest is using an abstract
world model as a prior to build a task-specific one on the fly, and they propose
ARC-AGI-3-style games as the ideal testbed. Lee et al.~\cite{lee2024} give the
one direct precedent --- DreamerV3 beating PPO on multi-step ARC-AGI-1 tasks ---
and explicitly call for scaling to harder interactive tasks. This work is the
first model-based RL (MBRL) entry on ARC-AGI-3 and supplies the missing standard
RL entry point.

\section{Contributions}
\textbf{(1) Substrate (primary artifact).} The first Gymnasium- and
DreamerV3-\texttt{embodied}-compatible wrapper for ARC-AGI-3. It exposes a
4102-way flat discrete action space ($0$--$4$$\to$\texttt{ACTION1--5};
$5$--$4100$$\to$\texttt{ACTION6} over the $64\!\times\!64$ click grid via
\texttt{unravel\_index}; $4101$$\to$\texttt{ACTION7}/undo) with per-game boolean
action masking applied to the actor logits at reset; a $(64,64,3)$ \texttt{uint8}
image observation in DreamerV3's native modality; an offline loader for the
340-replay human-demonstration corpus; and a post-hoc RHAE harness. Round-trip
action coding, full-corpus loader parse, and RHAE are property-/regression-tested
(318-test suite green). DreamerV3 is used \emph{stock and unforked} (config
\texttt{size12m}, the ARC-AGI-1-precedented choice~\cite{lee2024}); the wrapper
registers as an \texttt{embodied} environment with no upstream modification.

\textbf{(2) Controlled negative result.} A paired baseline of stock DreamerV3 on
6 public games $\times$ 2 seeds, evaluated from-scratch (cold) vs.\ warm-started
from a cross-game world model pretrained on all 340 human replays mixed across
25 games (Regime B: shared WM, per-game actor). Cross-game WM pretraining yields
no measurable benefit within seed variance.

\textbf{(3) Mechanistic diagnosis.} The world model's reconstruction and
dynamics losses collapse to convergence while RHAE stays $\approx$0: the model
\emph{learns to predict the environment}, but the controller cannot exploit it
inside imagination within a 500k-step budget.

\section{Method}
World model (encoder + RSSM + decoders + reward/continue heads) was pretrained
self-supervised on the full mixed 340-replay buffer (Phase~3, 1$\times$A100; image
reconstruction loss $53.66\!\to\!0.14$, dynamics loss monotone then flat at
$\sim$1.12). For each game the pretrained WM is loaded as initialisation, a fresh
actor and critic are created, the per-game human replays are pre-populated, and
online interaction proceeds for 500k env-steps with default DreamerV3
hyperparameters. The from-scratch (cold) arm is identical except the warm-start
flag is removed --- so warm-start is the \emph{only} difference between arms,
giving a clean ablation of the central Regime-B premise. Human baselines for
RHAE use the upper-median of first-time-player action counts; levels with
$<2$ completers are dropped from both numerator and denominator (resulting
human-baseline coverage 70.49\%, 129/183 levels over 25 games).

\vspace{-0.4em}
Table~\ref{tab:diag} localises the failure: the world model is \emph{not} the
bottleneck (its imagination is as accurate as the data permits and has
plateaued); the controller is. This split is \emph{not} a vc33 artifact. Across
all six pilot warm cells (\{vc33, sb26, cd82\}$\times$2 seeds) the
image-reconstruction loss collapses $>$95\% (final $0.14$--$1.9$), yet the two
gate-failure games clear $0/24$ eval episodes in \emph{every} cell. The sharpest
case is cd82, where the WM fits \emph{tighter} than on vc33 (image loss
$\to 0.06$) while the controller holds a $1.000$ random-action fraction for the
entire run --- the exploration$\to$exploitation schedule never even begins to
move. The WM-fits/controller-fails signature is the rule, not one lucky run.

\section{Discussion and positioning}
The result is the first MBRL data point on ARC-AGI-3 and is consistent with,
and sharpens, the survey's $\sim$3$\times$ cliff~\cite{survey2026}: a model that
provably fits these environments still cannot act in them under a stock
controller and a realistic compute budget, which is exactly the
``static-representation vs.\ on-the-fly task model'' gap Ying et al.\
predict~\cite{ying2025}. It also qualifies the Lee et al.~\cite{lee2024}
precedent: DreamerV3's ARC-AGI-1 success does \emph{not} transfer to the
interactive, rule-inference regime of ARC-AGI-3 at this scale, and cross-game WM
pretraining does not close the gap --- isolating the controller, not the world
model, as the locus of difficulty. \textbf{Limitations:} a compute-bounded
500k-step budget and $N=6$ games $\times$ 2 seeds --- a scoped claim about
\emph{stock DreamerV3 at this budget $\pm$ this pretraining}, not that MBRL
cannot do ARC-AGI-3; the diagnosis, not the headline number, carries the weight.
\textbf{Status \& next steps:} Phases 0--4 complete; the substrate is durable
and reusable independent of any result. Phase~6 (linear probes on RSSM latents,
FVD, reasoning-axis stratification) will quantitatively confirm the
localisation; controller-side interventions (raise \texttt{train\_ratio};
faster entropy decay) are the identified, supervisor-gated levers.
